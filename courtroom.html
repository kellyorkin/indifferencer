The Corporate Courtroom: The Debriefings is a standalone adversarial card game set in the most dangerous arena known to professional life: a corporate legal proceeding in which everyone has an agenda, no one has all the facts, and the client had a simple question two years ago that somehow ended up here.

A companion to Meetings: The Blathering, The Debriefings shares its universe of resigned corporate absurdism but introduces a tighter, more gameable structure. Where Meetings sprawls — because meetings sprawl — a courtroom has a beginning, a middle, and an end.

Someone wins. Someone loses. The chaos resolves. The client still does not know the answer to their original question. This is the cathartic counterpoint.

Procedure

Rules of the Court

01
Camp System
Prosecution and Defense function as opposing camps — each with internal hierarchy, aligned incentives, and the ability to play cards across the aisle. The adversarial relationship is structural, not incidental. Neutral cards serve the court itself. They can be activated by either camp under the right conditions.
02
Verdict Tokens
At the end of each round, the camp with higher total ATK wins the round and places one Verdict Token in the jury box. First to three wins. The Jury Foreperson holds the token physically. Control of the Foreperson is a legitimate strategic target. The client is not a target. The client is simply there.
03
Cross-Examination
Force your opponent to reveal their full hand. You choose one card to discard. The person who walked in confident leaves visibly diminished. Initiatable once per round by the Prosecutor. This is the stakeholder meeting. This is the intended outcome.
04
The Product Lifecycle Trilogy
Subpoena → Contempt → Evidence → Appeals. Played in sequence, each card buffs the next. Completing the chain triggers The Full Lifecycle: opponent discards two cards, the round extends one additional turn. Nobody remembers what the product was for. The client still does not know the answer to their original question.
