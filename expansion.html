About This Expansion

The Base Set Documents the Office.
This Documents The Floor.

The convention circuit is its own ecosystem — a place where spectacle substitutes for substance, where vendor credibility is measured in booth square footage, and where the Business Analyst Boar eats a sad sandwich from the convention center while watching his findings get rebranded by a flock of Parrots.

Tradeshows: The Corporate Circus introduces Booth Space as a new resource, a dedicated convention floor environment, and cards that can only be summoned in that environment. Bigger booth. More impressive cards. Completely unclear ROI.

The Platform and Data Lion Tamer finds her true home here — in the place where the circus never stopped being a circus, it just got a badge and a lanyard.

New Resource Mechanic
Booth Space
A resource representing literal square footage on the convention floor. More Booth Space enables more impressive Tradeshow cards. Booth Space costs real Bandwidth to acquire. ROI is always unclear.

Players begin with 0 Booth Space. It must be purchased during Main Phase using the Convention Floor field spell.
1 Booth Space = 200 Bandwidth
Maximum Booth Space: 20
Unused Booth Space at end of duel: Counts for nothing.

Convention Floor Environments

Where the Action (Doesn't) Happen

The Main Floor
The primary convention environment. Enables Booth Space purchases. Tradeshow cards may only be summoned here. Noise level prevents Documentation Checks from being heard clearly — all verification effects require one additional turn to resolve.
The VIP Lounge
Accessible only to holders of the All Access Badge of Unearned Privilege. All Bandwidth costs reduced by 30%. All deals made here are assumed to have happened even without documentation. Cannot be entered by any card with ATK below 2000.
The Keynote Hall
During the Keynote Hall environment, all players must face forward. The active Thought Leader Tier™ card's effects are doubled. All other cards' effects are halved. Duration: exactly as long as it needs to be, which is longer than scheduled.
The Breakout Room
Twelve chairs. Forty registrants. When this environment activates, randomly select 28 players who cannot participate this turn. The remaining twelve draw one card. Somehow this is considered a success.
